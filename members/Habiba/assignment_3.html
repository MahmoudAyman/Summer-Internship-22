3D Print Project: Windup Toy Car

Introducing the first real zero-emmtion car no fule or electricity needed. Just spin and playðŸ˜‹

Programs Required

  • CAD program (preferably SolidWorks)
  • Ultimaker Cura software program

Material used

  • 19g, 2.4 meter fillament
  • 4h 4min printing time

Step 1 - Download the Design

Go to the Google Drive link here to download every part of the toy car

Step 2 - Customize your Car

  1. Open the file named "Capo" in SolidWorks
  2. Open a sketch and select the top plane
  3. Sketch the desired shapes or works you want on the hood of the car
    4. In the picture below you'll see that I added Fiat 128, because this toy model looked like the old 128 cars that I see roaming the street in Egypt.
  4. Select Featueures > Extrueded Boss/Base.
  5. Click OK
  6. Open up new sketch and select the front plane
  7. Sketch two headlights in the front like in this picture below
  8. Select Featueures > Extrueded Boss/Base.
  9. Press OK
  10. Make sure to save the file as .STL

Step 3 - Setup Ultimaker Cura

  1. Open the STL files in Ultimaker
  2. Select the part and resize it as desired (here I resized each part to 80% its original size)
  3. On the bottom right corner press the slice button, it will give you deatails on the durration of the printing and the material used
  4. Save the file as GCODE and put it in the SD of the 3D printing machine
  5. Insert the SD card and begin printing
