Circuit Diagram

555 Timer ICU

This ICU can be used as a timer, ocillator or flip-flop. It operated from 4.5 to 16 Volts and can directly drive LEDs and relays.

In this project it is used as a timer to coordinate the durration of LED on and off phases.

How it Works

After we supply this circuit with power the green LED will turn on, because the voltage at pin 6 is less than the supply voltage. The red LED can not power on yet but the second ICU is powered so the green LED turns on.

The capacitor slowly charges and as soon as it charges to 2/3 of the supply voltage, the supply voltage of the second IC turns off and the yellow LED turns on (the ICU is discharging). It keeps glowing until the supply of the capacitor reaches 2/3 of the supply voltage

Simultaneously, the capacitor of the first IC reacges grater than 2/3 of the supply voltage, so the output of the first ICU turns off. This results in the yellow LED turning off and the red LED turning on.

The red LED is connected only when the output of the first ICU is 0V because it is connected to the positive voltage. The Yellow LED turns on during the charging mode of the second ICU. Assembly

  1. Use two jumpers to connect positive power rails with the other side and the negative power rails to each other, so that both sides of the breadbord will be supplied woth voltage upon inserting.
  2. Place the first 555 IC on the DIP support with the notch facing the left side.
  3. Connect pin 8 to positive rail and pin 1 to negative rail. Below is a sketch illustrating the the pin numbers.
  4. Connect pins 2 & 6 together.
  5. Connect pins 4 & 8 together.
  6. Place 100kÎ© Resistor between pins 6 & 3.
  7. Place one capacitor between pin 2 and negative rail (positive side to pin 2 and negative to negative rail.
  8. Place the second 555 IC futher way from the fist one on the DIP support with the notch facing the left side.
  9. Connect pin 3 of the first IC to pin 8 of the second (now the second ICs' power supply comes from the fist IC)
  10. Connect pin 1 to negative rail.
  11. Connect pins 2 & 6 together.
  12. Connect pins 4 to positive rail.
  13. Place 330kÎ© Resistor between pins 6 & 3.
  14. Place the other capacitor between pin 2 and negative rail (positive side to pin 2 and negative to negative rail.
  15. Insert the red and the yellow LEDs, so that the anode sides are on the same terminal strips but the cathode sides are in two different strips.
  16. Connect their anod sides to the positive rail
  17. Connect pin 3 of the first IC to the cathode side of the red LED using a 330Î© resistor
  18. Connect the cathode side of the yellow LED to pin 7 of the second IC using a 330Î© resistor
  19. Place a green LED on the bread board, connect its anode side to pin 3 of the second IC and its cathode side to negative rail using a 180Î© resistor

Note that you can change the time by chaning the value of the resistance and/or the capasitors.
